About

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is the space agency of the Government of India. It is responsible for India's space research and exploration efforts, including the development and launch of satellites, planetary exploration missions, and various space-related projects. ISRO has achieved significant milestones, including launching missions to Mars and the Moon, and has been a pioneer in providing affordable satellite launch services for international clients.

23 Jun 2022

GSAT-24

The GSAT-24, a communication satellite, was launched by India on June 23, 2022, from Kourou, French Guiana. Weighing 4 tonnes, it has 24 Ku-band transponders, offering nationwide DTH coverage. Built by ISRO and leased to Tata Play, a private DTH operator, this marks NSIL's first demand-driven satellite mission post-Indian space sector reforms. GSAT-24 is set to improve the quality and reach of Tata Play's broadcasting services in India.

August 12th 2021

GSLV-F10

The GSLV-F10/EOS-03 space mission by India failed on August 12, 2021, aiming to deploy the EOS-03 satellite (GISAT-1) into a geostationary orbit. EOS-03 was designed for near real-time Earth imaging. Using a GSLV rocket with a new payload fairing and a cryogenic engine, the mission faced technical issues in the cryogenic stage, resulting in a mission failure, the first GSLV failure since 2010. ISRO is investigating the cause and planning a future backup satellite launch.

November 27th 2020

PSLV-C49

On November 7, 2020, India's PSLV-C49 successfully launched the EOS-01 satellite. EOS-01 is an Earth observation satellite that was placed into orbit by the PSLV-C49 mission. This satellite is designed for various Earth monitoring tasks, such as agriculture, forestry, and disaster management. The PSLV-C49 mission was a significant achievement in India's space program, further enhancing its capabilities in Earth observation and remote sensing.
